The Strength of Intellectual Property

What's an Intellectual Property? What's a Brand?

IP - the product you provide to the market.
Brand - the company and how they interact with the market.

IP Builder - create a uniquely emotional experience with gameplay, characters, world, story, art, etc.
Brand Builder - communicate that emotional experience to the market that is impactful, stands out, and appeals to the target audience. They make a promise.

Human beings prefer things they already have an emotional relationship with. Thatâ€™s why all the top-selling games are sequels or spin-offs that are a part of an existing brand. Examples: Mario, Sonic, Lara Croft, Harry Potter.

Start with an IP and build around it. IP needs to have unique, artistic, emotional intent. Have something you own, all the best ips are character based.

But, you also need to balance creativity with business. Have a long-term sustainable goal for your game and company. Plan for success.

How to combat Game Dev Tunnel Vision?

Unbiased Market Research will tell you if your marketing tactics are working (A/B Testing).
Devs get too invested in the idea, but the market does not see the same value. What parts appeal to the audience? You have to think about how to communicate the experience of a new IP to new people. The market does not have the same relationship with the game as the developers currently do.

Marketing to hardcore audience (selling a game Genre) is different than marketing to a mainstream audience (selling an game IP). If you innovate too much, people wonâ€™t get it. Do something really well and innovate a little. Interaction is a story. Gameplay mechanics are a story. What experience does it give me?

Creating a lasting Brand

Transition from selling gameplay, to selling emotional experiences through a series of games. A strong IP can migrate to other media. Then, it becomes an Entertainment Property. Investors are thinking about this when you pitch.

Sometimes the Dev Team becomes the Brand. People trust their capabilities to deliver quality games. Building an IP and/or Brand improves future market success. Indies that have to compete against IPs and Brands, are put at a disadvantage.
